Assumptions & Limitations
1Photos taken by modern smartphones are very large files. This app automatically compresses photos to a maximum of 1920px and 85% quality before storing them. This is necessary to keep the exported ZIP file at a manageable size. Original photos on your device are not affected.
2This app has been tested with up to 100 site photos per report. If your site visit requires more than 100 photos, we recommend transferring the full photo dump separately via USB cable, OneDrive, or another file transfer method rather than through this app.
3This app is designed to work offline once loaded in your browser. Safeguards are in place to prevent data loss from accidental page refreshes. However, bugs may still occur — especially with very large photo sets. Always export your report before closing the browser.
4All information entered into this app stays on your device. No data is transmitted to any server. Your report, observations, and photos are only accessible to you until you choose to export and share them.
5On the report generator side, the folder selection prompt for exporting site photos is not supported in Firefox. Firefox users can still download a correctly named ZIP file, but will need to manually unzip it to the correct location. Chrome and Edge provide a more seamless workflow.
6After generating the Word document, review the entire report carefully. Verify that all text transferred correctly, photos are in the right locations, and captions are accurate. This is especially important if voice-to-text was used to capture any fields or observations, as dictation errors may not be immediately obvious.
7Signature placement relative to report content can vary depending on signature used. Always review signature locations after generating the report template and adjust manually as needed before finalizing.
8Your field data and observation notes are automatically saved as a draft and will survive an accidental page refresh. However, photos (both observation photos and site photos) are not saved to the draft due to device storage limitations. If the page is refreshed or you return to the Home screen, photos will need to be re-added.
9The Report # field expects a whole number (e.g. 1, 2, 3). If you need to use a different format, tap the Override button that appears. Voice-to-text number words such as "one" or "two" will be automatically converted to digits.
10When you tap Export, the ZIP file is downloaded to your device's Downloads folder or Files app. From there, you will need to transfer it to your office computer manually — via OneDrive, email (for smaller reports), USB cable, or another method of your choosing.
11The report generator requires a pre-configured Word template (.docx) with the correct placeholder fields. If the template is missing or outdated, the generated report may be incomplete or incorrectly formatted.
12DO NOT TAKE PHOTOS FROM WITHIN THIS APPLICATION. Use the camera app on your device to ensure the photos are stored. Any photos taken directly through this app are not saved to your Camera Roll and will be lost on refresh.
